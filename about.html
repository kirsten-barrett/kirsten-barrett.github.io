About Kirsten

I am a graduate of WVU, where I earned my degree in Interactive Design for Media and a minor in General Business. My studies covered a range of topics including: fine arts, web design, user experience, user research, game design, interaction design, animation, human-computer interaction, graphic design and video editing.


I have experience working in a...

  • Digital marketing agency as a Web UI Developer
  • Game development studio as a UX Designer and Project Manager
  • Digital marketing agency abroad as a UX/UI Design Intern

There's a gap on my resume...

I made a strategic decision to prioritize self-improvement and enhance my user experience skills rather than continue on a web development path focused on SEO. While I appreciate the importance of SEO, I realized my potential reached beyond just optimizing static sites for search engines. I gave my previous employer ample notice of my decision, and committed fully to mastering UX design. Now, I am eager to leverage my newly acquired skills and jump back into the job market.


Here's what I learned during my hiatus...

  • The essentials of effective information architecture
  • Feedback mechanisms and loops
  • The work of Don Norman and how I can best implement his principles
  • Best practices for documenting case studies & organizing research
  • Industry trends & more!
