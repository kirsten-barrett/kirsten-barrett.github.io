Transforming a One-Page Website into a Comprehensive Business Platform

Service Company Website


—


Project Overview

The client, a small family-owned company, approached me with the goal of transforming their basic one-page website into a comprehensive, functional platform that could better represent their growing business needs. My role involved both UX design and web development, allowing me to take full ownership of the project from conceptualization to execution. The final website needed to align with the client’s brand aesthetic while delivering an optimized and seamless user experience across all devices.


Project Goals

The key objectives for the project were:

  • Implement a user-friendly platform that simplifies user interaction and provides clear paths to contact the company
  • Provide detailed information about the company's services and integrate public health knowledge in a visually engaging manner
  • Encourage user engagement by driving conversions through calls or texts to schedule service or request more information

Research
& Discovery

—



Client Vision & Brand

My first step was to conduct an in-depth discussion with the client to understand their business, target audience, and vision for the site. This helped identify their brand message, values, and the overall aesthetic they wanted to convey. Given their family-owned business background, it was important that the site feel both approachable and professional, reflecting their credibility and customer-centric approach.




Competitor Analysis

To ensure the website stood out in the market, I performed a competitor analysis. This involved researching similar service providers to understand:

  • How they structured their websites
  • What kind of user experiences they provided
  • Gaps in the UX that we could improve upon

Key findings from competitor analysis:

  • Many competitor websites lacked intuitive navigation, which often frustrated users when searching for specific services or contact details
  • Content on competing sites was either too sparse or too dense, making it hard for users to quickly understand the offerings



User Personas

I created two primary user personas to guide design decisions and content organization.

Sarah - A Busy Professional

  • Age: 35
  • Goals: Needs quick access to services and clear contact options. Prefers websites with minimalistic design and concise information
  • Pain Points: Frustrated by cluttered sites that take too long to navigate; lacks the time to sift through irrelevant information

John - A Homeowner Looking for Trust

  • Age: 50
  • Goals: Wants detailed information about services, past client work, and customer reviews to establish trust
  • Pain Points: Unclear pricing, confusing navigation, and lack of contact details create friction in reaching out to service providers

These personas helped ensure the website met the needs of different user types, emphasizing both efficiency and detailed, valuable content. This put a focus on straight-to-the-point information.




Pain Points

  • Limited content on the one-page site made it hard for users to understand the full breadth of the client's services
  • No obvious calls-to-action (CTAs) were present to drive conversions such as phone calls, emails, or service bookings
  • The existing site did not offer mobile responsiveness, causing usability issues on mobile devices
  • Although the site listed their certifications, it was spare and unprofessional leading to a lack of trust

Solution
& Design Process

—



Site Architecture & Organization

One of the most significant improvements was transitioning from a single-page website to a multi-page structure. The new site architecture provided clear, easy-to-follow navigation.




Wireframes & Prototypes

Using Figma, I developed low-fidelity wireframes to outline the website's structure and key user flows, ensuring a seamless experience from one page to another. After client approval, I developed a quick prototype using WordPress and Elementor to provide a more tangible experience of the site's design and functionality.




Style Guide & Visual Design


The client had a blue and red color scheme tied to their logo, so I incorporated these colors throughout the design as accents, ensuring they were bold yet balanced. I maintained a minimalistic style to prevent the colors from overwhelming the user, focusing on clean typography and plenty of white space to enhance readability.


We also used high-quality images from the client's portfolio of recent projects. These visuals helped create a relatable experience, allowing potential customers to see the company's work firsthand and build trust.

Prototyping
& Development

—



Technical Considerations

I chose WordPress with an Elementor theme for its flexibility and ease of use. This ensured that the client could manage their website in the future without needing extensive technical knowledge.


Development involved:

  • Performance optimization: Image compression, CSS/JS minification, and lazy loading for fast load times
  • SEO improvements: On-page SEO techniques like meta descriptions, optimized titles, quality content, and schema markup
  • Mobile responsiveness: Ensuring that the site delivered a consistent, fully functional experience across all screen sizes



Testing & Launch

Before launching the site, I conducted rigorous testing:

  • Browser compatibility across Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge
  • Performance testing using Google Lighthouse, with a focus on improving load times and page speed
  • Accessibility checks to meet WCAG standards, ensuring the site was inclusive for all users

After thorough testing, the site went live. I also provided the client with training and documentation to manage and update the website independently.

Results
& Conclusion

—



The final website achieved all the set goals:

  • Simplified user experience: The multi-page structure made navigation easier, helping users find the information they needed quickly
  • Clear, compelling CTAs: The addition of clear contact options resulted in a 30-40% increase in phone calls and form submissions in the first 90 days
  • Responsive design: The website functioned seamlessly across devices, improving the mobile experience and driving engagement

By transforming the client's basic one-page site into a dynamic, user-friendly platform, we not only enhanced their online presence but also set the stage for future business growth. The new website reflected the company’s evolving needs while keeping the user at the center of the design process. The owner was thrilled with the outcome, expressing pride in how the website represented their brand. Through close collaboration with the client and a focus on UX best practices, we created a platform that effectively balanced aesthetics and functionality, and it was incredibly rewarding to see how proud the owner was of their new digital presence.
